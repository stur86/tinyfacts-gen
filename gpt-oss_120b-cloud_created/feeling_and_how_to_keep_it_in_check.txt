Feeling is a thing that appears in the mind and body. It can be strong or soft, bright or dark. A feeling can flash a burst of hot, or move like a slow line. It may come from a memory, a thought, a sound, a smell, or a sight.

To manage a feeling, first name it. Naming a feeling helps the mind see it as a thing. Next, let the feeling be, without fighting. Try to breathe slow and deep. A deep breath can calm the body and mind. If a feeling is too loud, try a short walk or a move.
Talk to a friend or write the feeling on paper. Seeing that the feeling is just a thing can make it less hard. Rest, eat, and get enough sleep. A good rest can make the mind new.

Feeling is part of our day. It helps us know what we like and what we need. By naming, breathing, moving, talking, and resting we can keep the feeling from take too much space in the mind or body.
