Why We Sleep

Sleep is something every animal needs. We spend about a third of our lives doing it, but for a long time we didn't really know why.

Here is what we think happens. During the day, your brain takes in so much - things it saw, heard, and felt, things you learned. When you sleep, the brain goes through everything it picked up and decides what to keep and what to throw away. This is why sleeping after you learn something helps you remember it better.

Sleep is also when your body does most of its work fixing things. When you cut yourself, the skin pulls back together while you rest. Children grow more while they sleep than while they are up and moving about. The brain also gets cleaned - stuff that builds up during the day gets wiped away.

When you go without sleep, things go wrong fast. After just one night, your brain decides things worse and your body feels more pain. After a few days without sleep, you start to see and hear things that aren't really there.

Dreams happen during one part of sleep where your eyes move quickly from side to side under their covers. No one is completely sure why we dream. Some think it's the brain trying to make sense of things that happened. Others think it's more like working through hard situations while you're safe in bed.

Most people need about seven to ten hours of sleep a night. But this changes with age - babies need much more, and older people often sleep less. Animals also change a lot in how much they need: some can sleep standing up, and some seem to go without sleep for long times with no problem at all.
