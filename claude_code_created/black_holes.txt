Dark Places In Space

A dark place in space is a spot where the pull is so strong that nothing can escape - not even light. Because light can't get out, these places look completely black against the sky.

It starts with a big star. When a big star gets old and runs out of the stuff that makes it burn hot, it falls in on its own, all that matter becomes very small but stays very heavy. The pull it makes on things around it becomes so strong that nothing - not rock, not air, not light - can get away.

If you were to fall toward one of these dark places, things would get very strange. Time would move in a different way for you than for someone watching from far away. The pull would be stronger on the side of you closest to it than on the side farthest away, so you would slowly be pulled long in the direction of the fall.

These dark places can be very different in how big they are. The smallest are about as big as a city. The largest - which sit in the middle of most big groups of stars - can be as heavy as very many suns put together.

We can't see these dark places with our eyes, but we can see how they change things around them. Stars near one move in strange ways. Some pull in air and rock that are close to them, which gets very hot and bright as it falls in, letting us know something strange is there.

What happens at the very middle, where all the matter is pushed into a point too small to see? We don't know. The ways we use to explain how things work seem to break down there.
