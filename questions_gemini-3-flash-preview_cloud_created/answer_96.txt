All things we see need light. Light comes from the sun. This light looks white, but it has all the colors of the world inside it. When light hits a thing, some colors stay in the thing. Other colors jump back. If the blue light hits your eyes, you see the thing as blue.

Blue is the color of the sky on a bright day. The air is full of tiny things. When light hits these tiny things, the blue light moves around more than the red or green light. Because of this, when you look up, you see blue all across the sky.

Large areas of water also look blue. The water keeps the red light inside it. The blue light can move through the water and come back to your eyes. Deep water looks more blue than water that is not deep.

Some animals that fly are blue. Often, they do not have blue color on their bodies like paint. Instead, the things on their bodies are built in a different way. The tiny parts make the light jump back as blue. If you broke those tiny parts into bits, the blue color would disappear.

Sometimes a person says they feel blue. They do not mean their body is the color of the sky. It means they feel sad. They might want to cry or stay in bed. When they feel better, they are not blue anymore.
