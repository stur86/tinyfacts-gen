To blood a person or an animal is a thing people do in old ways. It means to let a person or animal see or feel a fight for the first time.

In the old days, when men went out to kill animals for food, they would take a young person with them. If the young person helped kill their first animal, an older man would take the warm red stuff from the animal and put it on the face of the young person. This was to show that the young person was now a real part of the group. It was a way to bring them in. 

People also use this word for dogs or horses. If a dog is blooded, it has killed an animal before. This makes the dog want to do it more. For horses, blooded means the horse comes from a family of very fast and strong horses. People keep a book of their family names so they know the horse is one of the best.

We also say a person is blooded when they start a hard job or a fight. It means they finally know what the work is really like. They have seen the hard parts. It is not always about the red stuff inside our bodies anymore. It is about getting used to something that is not easy.
