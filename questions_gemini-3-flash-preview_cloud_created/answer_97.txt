To "blue" something or someone can mean many different things.

One meaning is to be sad. If someone has "the blues," or if you "blue" someone, it means they feel very bad inside. They are not happy and might want to cry. People say this because the color blue can feel cold, like deep water or the sky when the sun is gone.

Another way to "blue" something is to change how it looks. People "blue" things made of hard grey stuff to keep them from getting old. This is often done to guns or parts of a car. They make the outside look dark blue or black. This stops the air and water from turning the grey stuff into tiny red bits that fall away. You can also "blue" your white clothes to make them look even whiter.

You can also "blue" your money. This means you spend all your money very fast on things you do not really need.

If you "blue" some words in a book, you take them out. People might do this when they do not want others to read those words.

Finally, if something happens "out of the blue," it means it was a surprise. It happened very quickly and you did not expect it. It is like a flash of light in the night sky that you did not know was coming.
