When you blink, you close your eyes and open them again very quickly. This happens many, many times every single day. Most of the time, you do not even think about it. Your body just does it for you.

Your eyes need to stay wet to work well. There is a tiny bit of water that covers them. If your eyes stay open too long, that water goes away and your eyes get dry. When you blink, your eyes are covered in that water again. This keeps your eyes feeling good and helps you see well.

Blinking also guards your eyes. If something small flies toward your face, or if there is a lot of small stuff in the air, your eyes shut fast. This keeps the bad things out. If a light is too bright, you may blink a lot or keep your eyes closed to stay safe.

Sometimes, you blink more when you are tired. If you look at a computer for a long time, you might forget to blink as much as you should, and then your eyes feel bad. It is important to let your eyes rest.

You can also blink because you want to. You can try to not blink for a long time, but eventually, your eyes will start to hurt, and your body will force them to shut for a moment.
